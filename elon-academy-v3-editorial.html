Chapter Two · The Gap

Nationally, the odds
are stacked.

Across the country, only 14 percent of low-income students graduate from college. Just a third even enroll. Talent is everywhere — opportunity is not.

The Elon Academy was built to close that gap for Alamance County: students with financial need and/or no family history of college, chosen for their academic promise and given a real path.

Chapter Three · The Program

Five programs,
one promise.

i.

Summer Academy

Three consecutive summers living and learning on Elon's campus — before sophomore, junior, and senior years.

ii.

Saturday Academy

Year-round Saturday sessions for scholars and families: academics, college prep, community.

iii.

Academic Coaching

One-on-one coaching that keeps scholars on track in their toughest courses.

iv.

College Mentors

Current college students guide scholars through applications, essays, and decisions.

v.

College Success

Support continues on each scholar's college campus — until the diploma is in hand.

05
Chapter Five · The Proof

The record speaks
plainly.

Every high school scholar takes at least one honors or AP class. The average unweighted GPA is 3.66. One hundred percent are accepted to colleges and universities, and 96 percent stay in North Carolina.

Eleven scholars have earned the nation's most competitive merit awards — Gates-Millennium, Golden Door, Morehead-Cain, and Cheatham-White scholarships. The Class of 2023 alone earned more than $5.2 million in merit-based aid.

Chapter Six · The Ask

This story runs on
generosity — not tuition.

The Elon Academy was launched by Elon University, but it isn't funded by tuition dollars. Every chapter of every scholar's story is underwritten by individuals, businesses, and foundations who believe promising students deserve the chance to write one.
