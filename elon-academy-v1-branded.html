What is the Elon Academy?

Part of Elon University's Center for Access and Success, the Elon Academy is a non-profit program serving students with financial need and/or no family history of college. Scholars get three summer residential experiences on Elon's campus, year-round Saturday programs, and support all the way through college graduation.

🏫

Free to Families

The Academy is not funded through tuition — it runs on the generosity of individuals, businesses, and foundations.

🎓

Academically Promising

Every high school scholar takes at least one honors or AP class, with an average unweighted GPA of 3.66.

🤝

Whole-Family Support

Families participate alongside scholars through Saturday programs and the Transitions to College program.

Programs

Five connected programs guide each scholar from sophomore year of high school through a college degree.

☀️

Summer Academy

Three consecutive summers living and learning on Elon's campus before sophomore, junior, and senior years.

📅

Saturday Academy

Year-round Saturday sessions for scholars and families — academics, college prep, and community.

📚

Academic Coaching

One-on-one coaching that keeps scholars on track in their toughest courses.

🧑‍🎓

College Mentors

Current college students mentor scholars through applications, essays, and big decisions.

🚀

College Success

Support continues after high school graduation — all the way to a college diploma.

The Scholar Journey

A multi-phase path that starts freshman year of high school and ends with a college degree.

Apply — Freshman Year

Academically promising students in Alamance County apply and are selected as scholars.

Three Summer Residencies

Scholars live on Elon's campus each summer before sophomore, junior, and senior years.

Year-Round Saturday Programs

Scholars and families build skills and community throughout the school year.

Transitions to College

The summer after graduation, scholars and families prepare for the leap to campus life.

College Success Program

Continued support on their college campuses — 79% of scholars earn a college degree, versus 14% of low-income students nationally.

The Academy runs on generosity.

The Elon Academy is not funded through tuition dollars. Its future depends on individuals, businesses, and foundations who believe every promising student deserves a shot at college.
